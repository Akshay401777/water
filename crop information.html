Crop Information

Wheat

Scientific Name: Triticum

Description: Wheat is a staple food that is used to make bread, pasta, and many other products. It is one of the most widely cultivated crops in the world.

Temperature: 10-25°C

Humidity: 50-70%

Corn

Scientific Name: Zea mays

Description: Corn, also known as maize, is a cereal grain first domesticated by indigenous peoples in southern Mexico about 10,000 years ago. It is a major food crop and also used in animal feed and biofuel.

Temperature: 15-30°C

Humidity: 60-80%

Rice

Scientific Name: Oryza sativa

Description: Rice is a staple food for more than half of the world's population. It is cultivated in flooded fields known as paddies and is a key part of many cultures' diets.

Temperature: 20-35°C

Humidity: 70-90%

Potatoes

Scientific Name: Solanum tuberosum

Description: Potatoes are a versatile tuber used worldwide in various dishes. They are a staple food in many regions and are known for their high carbohydrate content.

Temperature: 15-20°C

Humidity: 80-90%

Tomatoes

Scientific Name: Solanum lycopersicum

Description: Tomatoes are a widely consumed fruit that is used in salads, sauces, and many other dishes. They are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

Temperature: 18-25°C

Humidity: 60-70%

Carrots

Scientific Name: Daucus carota

Description: Carrots are a root vegetable known for their high beta-carotene content. They are commonly eaten raw or cooked and are used in a variety of dishes.

Temperature: 10-20°C

Humidity: 60-80%

Onions

Scientific Name: Allium cepa

Description: Onions are a popular vegetable known for their strong flavor and aroma. They are used in a variety of cuisines around the world and have many health benefits.

Temperature: 13-25°C

Humidity: 65-80%

Spinach

Scientific Name: Spinacia oleracea

Description: Spinach is a leafy green vegetable rich in vitamins and minerals. It is commonly used in salads, soups, and various dishes for its nutritional benefits.

Temperature: 10-20°C

Humidity: 60-70%
